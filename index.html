About Me

My journey started one late night in the lab, staring at a spreadsheet that felt more overwhelming than insightful. That frustration sparked a bigger question: how can we use digital tools more effectively in science — and build ones that are easier to use, more intuitive, and genuinely helpful? Since then, I've been learning to do just that — combining my background in biology with growing skills in web development, UX, and data analysis.

As part of my Diploma in Full Stack Software Development, I've worked with technologies like Python, JavaScript, Django, React, and PostgreSQL to build applications that are functional, data-driven, and user-focused. Whether it's creating a front-end user experience or structuring backend logic, I've been especially drawn to projects that translate raw data into insights people can actually use.

My goal is to use data and technology to make science smarter, healthcare more responsive, and education more inclusive. I'm especially interested in working at the intersection of health, data, and innovation — where insights can shape better decisions and better experiences.

While I'm early in my tech journey, I bring a strong interdisciplinary mindset, a passion for evidence-based thinking, and the communication skills to connect ideas across fields. I'm excited to keep growing and to work on projects that use data to solve real problems.
